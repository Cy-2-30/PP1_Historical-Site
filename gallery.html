Origins

Khoekhoe is an ethnic designator. It refers to several populations which speak closely related languages and are historical pastoralist communities in the South African Cape region, through to Namibia, where Khoekhoe populations of Nama and Damara people are prevalent ethnicities.

Many Khoesān peoples are the direct descendants of a very early dispersal of anatomically modern humans to Southern Africa 150,000 years ago. Their languages show a vague typological similarity, largely confined to the prevalence of click consonants. They are not verifiably derived from a common proto language, but are today split into at least three separate and unrelated language families (Khoe-Kwadi, ǃUi-Taa and Kxʼa). It has been suggested that the Khoekhoe (Khoekhoe peoples) may represent Late Stone Age arrivals to Southern Africa, displaced by Bantu immigration.
